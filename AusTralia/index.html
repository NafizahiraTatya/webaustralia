Festival

Australia hosts a variety of festivals celebrating the diversity of culture, art, music, food and drink. Here are some famous festivals in Australia:

Sydney Festival

An annual arts festival taking place in Sydney, featuring performing arts, theatre, music, dance and various other cultural events.

Adelaide Festival

A multidisciplinary arts festival held in Adelaide, featuring theater, music, dance and visual arts performances from around the world.

Vivid Sydney

A festival of light, music and creative ideas takes place in Sydney, with spectacular light displays on the city's iconic buildings.

Taste of Tasmania

Food and drink festival taking place in Hobart, Tasmania, featuring local food, wine, beer and gourmet products.

Darwin Festival

An arts and culture festival held in Darwin, Northern Territory, featuring music, dance, theater and other cultural events.

Brisbane Festival

An arts and culture festival held in Brisbane, featuring spectacular art performances, music, dance and light displays.

Australian's Food

Discover Australian's food

Aboriginal diets are strongly influenced by the region in which they are located. Most tribal groups relied on a simple Paleolithic diet, they were hunter gatherers who hunted land animals or fish, and collected plants, and endemic fruits. The general term for Australian flora, and fauna used as a food source is bush tucker.

Australian's culture

Discover Australian's culture

Australian culture is characterized by diversity, outdoor lifestyle, sports, larrikinism, arts, food, national symbols, and a laid-back attitude. Influenced by Indigenous heritage, British colonial history, and multiculturalism, Australians enjoy outdoor activities, love sports like cricket and Australian Rules football, embrace a relaxed and humorous approach to life, and appreciate a vibrant arts scene.

Australian's sport

Discover Australian's sport

Approximately 24% of Australians over 15 regularly participate in organized sports. Australia has successful international teams in cricket, field hockey, netball, rugby league, and university rugby, winning world or Olympic championships at least twice in the last 25 years for both men and women.
